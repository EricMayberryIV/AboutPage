Who is this guy?

My name is Eric A. Mayberry IV, and I originally hail from the great state of Texas. At sometime in my life, I have lived in or near the four largest cities in Texas; San Antonio, Dallas (Plano actually), Austin, and Houston. I have also lived in 5 other states; Illinois, California, Virginia, Pennsylvania, and Florida. I am a former U.S. Navy Sailor (FC2 (SW)) , a former Industrial Electrician, a current Programming student at Florida Agriculture & Mechanical University (Computer Information Systems), and a current Program Liaison for the Florida Department of Environmental Protection.

What is he looking to accomplish?

Well, I guess I should start with a bit of a back story as to how I ended up as a programming student at this stage in my life. I have always been attracted to computers and information technology. This goes back, in my mind, to when I used to type out the free programs that they use to print in the back of Commodore Magazine during the (very) late 80's. Then I suppose, around 1999, when my mother shipped out a WebTV to me, I taught myself how to do some redimentary HTML. Around that time I was learing a lot about repairing PC's and doing what I could to teach myself how to fix common issues with software.

I thought that it was all just a hobby. I guess I resisted because I believed that if you turned what you did as a hobby into a job, you would end up hating it. OH...MY...GOODNESS, I couldn't have been more WRONG! Let me tell you now, if you find something that you love and can make money at it, it is not a "job" at all. I mean, I get paid to do what I absolutely LOVE to do.

Alright, so how did I specifically end up on a programming path? Well, that has everything to do with the fact that I attempted to get an Engineering degree. Once I had graduated from the local community college (TCC), I enrolled at FAMU to persue my Engineering degree. That's when I was placed in my first official OOP class, and instantly fell in love with programming.

Since that first class, I have been doing what I can to expand my knowledge of as many programming techniques and languages as I can. For the time being, I have been focusing on honing my skills in HTML5 and CSS3, while also learning as much as I can about JavaScript¹, JQuery¹, Ruby¹, ASP², VBScript², PHP, SQL, C++, and Shell. My ultimate goal is to work, either for myself or for a major corporation, coding websites and/or applications.

¹ Learning as I go, but it's a work in progress.
 ² Required for my current position.
